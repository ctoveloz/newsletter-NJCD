Torah Chapter Eika

Some people make a mistake. They think when a person continues to do bad they can erase the bad with some good. They think that although they continue to do wrong, when they do good Hashem just erases their evil and then everything is ok. This is not right. This weekâ€™s Torah Chapter teaches us a very important lesson about this.

It is written: Devorim 10:17-18 .... The Great G-D that does not show favor and does not take bribes. He carries out justice for the orphan, the widow etc. The Ksav Sofer (very great rabbi and Torah commentator) explains the following: Suppose a person is dishonest but very successful in his business. With his profits he gives a lot of money to help orphans and widows. This rich person giving a lot of money to help orphans and widows might think Hashem will forgive him for being dishonest. After all he is doing a big mitzvah. The Torah says: Hashem does not take BRIBES. Because his mitzvah of helping others comes from dishonesty this person will not protect himself self from punishment. First a person must stop being dishonest. Do the correct tshuvah (repentance). Only then he will receive the great reward of helping orphans and widows.

It is like taking a rotten apple and covering it with chocolate. It might look and taste good, but underneath it is still rotten. A person must first stop doing wrong. Then he will receive reward. Hashem does not take bribes
